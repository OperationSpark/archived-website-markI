About Operation Spark

Operation Spark is a New Orleans non-profit that teaches the fundamentals of software development in a fun and engaging environment. We’re opening a new door to prosperity for disconnected youth by presenting the fastest route to a career in software development.

Through our programs we progressively increase the skills of our students from novice to the level of internship. Part of our mission is to empower New Orleans schools with the literacy of software creation so we’re now offering courses tailored specifically for teachers in a K-12 environment.

What to expect

You'll learn the fundamentals of software development while using real tools, adopting best practices, and building actual web-based applications. Boot Camp takes the uninitiated to the novice level by ensuring they’re literate in the building blocks of the web: JavaScript, HTML and CSS.

By the end of Boot Camp, participants become familiar with the professional workflow of a software developer. Armed with your newfound knowledge of software you'll be able to teach your students how to create compelling apps and games.

Our Teacher Training begins with a one day crash course. We speed you through the tools you'll be using, our overall workflow, writing code, debugging, and general problem solving.

Following our crash course, we’ll see you once a week for a 3 hour session, where we’ll step through new lesson materials and support you through your experience in conducting your own Boot Camp. Once you're done we offer support through our mentorship program and our online forum where you can ask us questions and paste code. You’ll also be provided with curriculum and digital tools for managing the tasks and progress of each participant.

Boot camp deliverables

By end of camp, our participants are on their way to being super users, accessing computers via the command-line, reading from and persisting data to the file-system, and they leave literate in the basics of JavaScript having built several apps and games in Node.js and HTML5. They’ve learned to debug an app and problem solve on the Internet, and know where to find the resources to continue learning on their own or in teams. They also learn the soft skills that make them a great team player.
